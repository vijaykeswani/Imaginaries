This task mimics the labor of "ghost workers" around the world who label images as part of a low-paying, precarious “gig” job. Their labor produces what in AI is called “ground truth,” pointing to the construction of truth through the extractivist and exploitative logics of late capitalism—outsourcing, efficiency, and mass production. "Ground truth" takes on the imprint of this process—it incorporates the various perspectives of these ghost workers. Here we give up control of this key stage in knowledge production, disrupting the traditional power dynamics of representation in machine learning. Now this is in the hands of the user, who can see if the algorithm sees the world as they do.
